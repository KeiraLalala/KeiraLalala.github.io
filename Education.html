Education


Doctor of Philosophy (2020 - 2024)
Supervisors: Dr Roland Goecke & Dr Damith Herath

My thesis research focused on investigating how deep learning models can recognise and detect the micro- and macro-expressions, and explored the potential to assist in diagnosing depression. I developed a novel system that combines deep learning techniques and computer vision methods to preprocess source video data, normalise image samples, and extract significant expression features for final detection and classification.



The novelty of my research lies in the design of an end-to-end framework that is sensitive to subtle dynamic features, while effectively eliminating the effects of static facial characteristics. A key innovation of this work is the development of deep learning models that achieve high performance with limited data, overcoming the traditional challenge of requiring large datasets for training.



The final component of my PhD project involved spotting macro- and micro- expressions from long videos containing various intensities and time scales of facial movements. In other words, the system is designed to spot and extract significant information from noisy dynamic data.
